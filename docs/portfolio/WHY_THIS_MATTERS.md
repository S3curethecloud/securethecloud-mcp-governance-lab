# Why This Matters

AI assistants are becoming AI agents. Agents do not just generate text; they can call tools, query data, create records, send messages, and trigger workflows.

That creates a governance problem.

Before an AI agent can use enterprise tools, an organization needs to answer:

- Who is the user?
- What is the user's role and clearance?
- What tool is being requested?
- What data classification is involved?
- What is the business purpose?
- Does the request require human approval?
- What policy decision applies?
- Was the action allowed, blocked, redacted, escalated, or paused?
- Can the decision be replayed later as evidence?

The SecureTheCloud MCP Governance Lab demonstrates this control pattern.

It shows that AI can assist and recommend, but tool execution must pass through identity, policy, approval, firewall, and evidence controls first.

This is the difference between an impressive AI demo and an enterprise-ready AI governance story.
